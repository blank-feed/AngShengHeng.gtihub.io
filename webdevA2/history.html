Early History

  • Ten-Pin bowling is derived from Kegeling or more commonly known as Nine-Pin bowling.

  • However, Kegeling is associated with gambling and was banned. In order to avoid the ban, they added an additional pin and hence Ten-Pin bowling is found.

  • In less than half a century, bowling ball manufacturing companies have started producing such revolutionary bowling balls that it got displayed at the Century of Progress Exposition.

  • Bowling clubs have also formed the National Bowling Associate (NBA) to standardize rules and improve the sport's image.

1940s - 1980s

  • The first mechanical pinsetter was introduced, replacing human pinsetters.

  • The PBA, professional Bowlers Associate, was founded in 1958 by Eddie Elias.

  • Professional bowling was sponsored by beer companies, CoCa-CoLa and Ford too.

  • The "Lane Master", which is an automatic lane cleaning and conditioning machine, was first deployed in the 1960s.

  • Top Bowling Professionals made twice as much money as NFL Football Stars in around the 1960s.

1980s - Present Day

  • Ten-Pin Bowling became an exhibition sport. It is featured at events such as the 1988 Summer Olympics held in Seoul.

  • 1991 came the introduction of "The Lane Walkers". They are the first computer-driven lane cleaning and oiling machine and they can be used up to 50 lanes.

  • The development of reactive resin bowling balls with chemically tacky surfaces started around the early 1990s.

  • In 1997, the Sporting Goods Manufacturers Association (SGMA) reported around $215 million worth of bowling products sold.
