Plastic/polyester

Plastic (or polyester) coverstocks are the smoothest and the least reactive surface type on bowling balls.

They tend to not hook or hook a little bit at most and can go directly from the foul line straight to the pins.

They are best used for sparing, hence most commonly found on spare balls.

Ball shown : Team Storm - White Belmo

Urethane

Urethane surfaces are a step up from plastic coverstocks.

They usually react the earliest amongst all surfaces and hook a few boards more than a plastic would.

Bowlers prefer using urethane balls when the lanes are either beaten down or the pattern is a short one.

Ball shown : Fast Pitch

Solid Reactive

There are 3 types of reactive coverstocks and solid is one of them.

Solid reactive coverstocks reacts the earliest out of all 3 types and they absorb the oil the fastest.

Bowlers typically use it on fresh patterns or heavy volume patterns.

Ball shown : Axiom

Hybrid Reactive

There are 3 types of reactive coverstocks and hybrid is one of them.

Hybrid balls react slightly later than pearl balls but still skid across the lane more than a solid would usually.

They can be used on a variety of lane conditions as they are quite flexible.

Ball shown : Hyped Hybrid

Pearl Reactive

There are 3 types of reactive coverstocks and pearl is one of them.

Pearl balls skid across the lane the most compared to solid or hybrid balls.

Bowlers tend to lean towards pearl balls once the lane begins to break down as they need the length in their balls.

Ball shown : Phaze 4
